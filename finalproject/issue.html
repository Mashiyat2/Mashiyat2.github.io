Climate Change

It may seem like a problem to worry about later, but climate change's effects are worsening daily and happening now. However, it's never too late to start helping reduce such effects!

What is Climate Change?

As the United Nations states, "Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns." While these changes can be natural, human activities, such as burning fossil fuels, have been the main cause. Through such actions, greenhouse gas emissions are created which traps all this heat in our atmosphere.

The Greenhouse Effect

Like an actual greenhouse, on Earth, gases in the atmosphere, like carbon dioxide, trap heat, hence greenhouse gases. This helps maintain a certain temperature on Earth. However, human activities put more CO2 in the atmosphere, so more heat is trapped, and the Earth is warmer. This isn't good of course as drastic effects can include extreme weather, more wildfires, air pollution, increased sea level, etc. (Climate Kids Nasa).

Effects of Climate Change

Some effects that have already been observed include; glaciers have shrunk, loss of sea ice, an increase in sea level, heat waves, and droughts.
